# Posting formats and route strategy

Save into the brain as slug `posting-formats`, kind `formats`.

This doc tells the story desk and writers' room how to choose the shape of a
post. The key principle: not every post should be satirical, and not every
post needs generated images.

## Route choice principle

Choose the post format from the job the story is doing:

- If the story is useful ingredient, evidence, regulatory, or industry news,
  it can be serious and informative.
- If the story exposes a specific product's claim gap, evidence mismatch, dose
  mismatch, pricing problem, or PubMed-link sleight of hand, use semi-satirical
  receipt-led video.
- If the story needs step-by-step comparison or multiple receipts, consider an
  Instagram carousel.
- If the story is simple, visual, and explanatory, use a short HyperFrames
  video with clean text, citations, charts, or screenshots.
- If the story is a buyer pain point where the app visibly reduces the work,
  use an app resolver reel.
- If the source material is screenshots, product pages, labels, or papers, do
  not invent decorative images. Use the receipts.

## App resolver reels

Use this route when the best post is not "look at this product" but "this is
the problem the app helps you solve".

This is the gold-standard format from the menopause search-wall post.

Best for:

- Reddit/comment intelligence where many people reveal the same buying pain
- confusing search results, supplement aisles, product grids, labels, claims,
  badges, COAs, PubMed links, or anecdote-chasing
- stories where the app can show one concrete behaviour before the CTA
- conversion posts where the viewer should think: "that would save me work"

Core structure:

1. **Pain:** name the buyer state in human language.
2. **Proof:** show the confusing environment or receipt.
3. **Check:** teach the one useful question.
4. **App resolver:** show De-Influenced performing that check.
5. **Behaviour:** tell the viewer what to paste/check before buying.

Default line:

> Search gives options. De-Influenced checks claims.

Default method:

> product + dose + claim

Format rules:

- Route label: `app resolver reel`
- Usually `job: conversion`
- Usually `tier: 2`
- Usually `platform: instagram` / `format: ig_reel`, with a TikTok cut if it
  fits the current lane
- Asset strategy: `mixed`
- Liz asset needs: app screen recording, product/search/label screenshot, or
  a real receipt if the proof depends on exact reality
- Agent asset plan: crop/highlight real assets, build HyperFrames motion,
  create branded cards, captions, contact sheet, and review stills

Good visual treatment:

- real search wall, product page, label, or app recording
- slow pan over the messy environment
- sparse text overlays
- midpoint method card
- app screen before the CTA, not only a closing logo
- final CTA card: "Check before you buy" / "First analysis free"

Do not use:

- AI-generated product bottles
- fake app results
- fake PubMed screens
- generic lifestyle images
- broad industry-rant copy without a single buyer behaviour

Route fit signals:

- audience language includes exhaustion, confusion, trial and error, "has
  anyone tried", "worth it", "which one", "what do I buy", "can I trust this"
- the evidence problem can be turned into one check
- the app can visibly perform the check in under 10 seconds
- the CTA is a natural next action, not a bolted-on sales line

Gold example:

- Pain: "You're already exhausted."
- Proof: menopause supplement search wall.
- Check: "Was this product tested, at this dose, for this claim?"
- App resolver: screen recording of De-Influenced analysing a menopause product.
- CTA: "Paste the product, URL, or claim before you buy."

## Serious informative posts

Use this route for:

- ingredient research updates
- meta-analyses or trials that need plain-English interpretation
- ASA/FDA/regulatory or industry news
- market shifts, new claim trends, reformulations, or pricing patterns
- stories where the value is "here is what this means" rather than "look at
  this absurd product claim"

Preferred craft:

- HyperFrames video or clean carousel
- clear headline
- 2-4 evidence beats
- one caveat
- simple takeaway
- no forced punchline

Visuals:

- clean kinetic text
- chart, table, or number callout
- source screenshot only if it helps
- restrained colours and readable captions
- generated decorative imagery only if it clarifies the concept and does not
  pretend to be evidence

Route labels to use:

- `app resolver reel`
- `informational HyperFrames video`
- `ingredient explainer carousel`
- `industry news brief`
- `study context video`

## Semi-satirical product receipt videos

Use this route when the story is about a named product or claim and the receipt
trail is the point.

Good triggers:

- the product page links to PubMed papers that do not support the claim being
  made
- the product claim is broader than the paper
- the studied dose, population, ingredient form, or outcome differs from the
  product claim
- a label or advert uses scientific language without product-specific evidence
- price, dose, or serving math changes the story

Preferred craft:

- screenshot-stitch video
- product page claim on screen
- PubMed/link screenshot
- paper abstract or dose detail
- simple on-screen annotation
- dry VO that lands after the evidence

Visuals:

- Liz-provided screenshots, browser captures, product page crops, label crops,
  PubMed screenshots, tables, price crops
- no decorative generated images
- no generic supplement bottles
- no AI lifestyle scenes
- no fake paper screenshots

Route labels to use:

- `screenshot-stitch satire`
- `PubMed receipt video`
- `claim-vs-paper video`
- `dose-gap product audit`
- `performance hook plus receipts`

Asset requests:

- Ask Liz for screenshots when the product page, claim, or linked evidence
  needs to be shown exactly.
- If a source URL is available, the agent can capture simple receipts, but it
  must not replace real screenshots with generated imagery.
- Mark this route as `needs_liz_assets` or `mixed`, not `agent_can_create`,
  when the credibility depends on real product receipts.

## Performance hook plus receipts

Use this when the product claim is technical, but the audience needs an
immediate human reason to care.

Reference pattern: Liz's menopause supplement patches Reel
https://www.instagram.com/p/DZGIt7TsOn0/

What made it work:

- The opening visual was self-shot and instantly understandable, not polished
  stock or AI imagery.
- The text overlay named the category clearly, then asked a simple audience
  question.
- The caption made the fair position clear first: this was not an anti-HRT or
  anti-patch argument.
- The evidence critique was narrow: dose and patch-delivery evidence, not a
  broad dismissal of the whole category.
- The satirical energy came from the mismatch between the product promise and
  the cautious claim language.

Use this route when:

- the audience has lived experience of the category
- Liz can create a quick physical/person-led opener
- the receipts are still the main proof
- the post needs warmth before critique

Do not use generated imagery for this route. Use Liz-shot video, screenshots,
screen recordings, product page crops, PubMed/paper receipts, and simple
annotations.

## Carousels

Use carousels when the user needs to swipe through receipts or compare several
claims.

Good uses:

- claim -> what the paper says -> what the dose was -> what the product says
- ingredient evidence ladder
- "what this study does and does not prove"
- side-by-side product audit

Preferred craft:

- one idea per slide
- short slide text
- visible evidence source
- final slide with the practical takeaway

## Format mix

Do not over-route everything into satire.

The account should mix:

- serious ingredient or industry explainers
- semi-satirical product receipt videos
- carousels for multi-step evidence
- quick updates when the news is useful but not scandalous

If in doubt, ask: "Is the entertainment coming from a real receipt?" If yes,
satire can work. If no, make it useful and clear instead.
