# Paid Meta ad gold standard

Save into the brain as slug `meta-ad-gold-standard`, kind `formats`.

Status: proposed paid-ad gold standard for Liz review. Once Liz signs this off,
this file is the one true source for De-Influenced paid Instagram and Facebook
carousel ad templates. Draft storyboards, scripts, and renderer templates must
defer to this standard when they conflict.

## What this owns

This seed owns paid Meta carousel ads for De-Influenced app acquisition:

- Instagram feed carousel ads.
- Facebook feed carousel ads.
- Static direct-response carousel decks built for testing.
- App-check and first-check-free conversion creative.

This seed does not own:

- Organic Instagram evidence carousels. Use `carousel-templates`.
- TikTok Photo Mode proof carousels. Use `carousel-templates`.
- TikTok videos, Reels, Stories, or founder-led video ads.
- Long educational decks, report explainers, or methodology posts.

Paid Meta ads are not normal posts. They need a stated audience state, a
testable promise, a proof cue, an offer line, a landing destination, and final
review before spend.

## The golden rule

The ad does not exist to say supplements are bad. It exists to show the buyer a
cleaner way to decide before checkout.

Every paid carousel must land here:

> De-Influenced is the check layer between supplement marketing and the buyer's
> money.

If the ad only attacks gummies, influencers, luxury packaging, PubMed wallpaper,
or proprietary blends without showing the app as the practical resolution, it
fails.

## Required format

Format: `meta_ad_carousel`  
Platforms: Instagram feed, Facebook feed  
Canvas: `1080x1350`, `4:5`  
Default length: 3 slides  
Maximum length: 3 slides  
Export rule: one deck per platform ratio. Do not mix ratios inside a carousel.

Quality objective:

- Every approved paid carousel must export a Meta-native `1080x1350` PNG deck.
- Every approved paid carousel must also export a `2160x2700` 2x master deck.
- The `2160x2700` deck is the working quality master for archive, Canva import,
  resizing, and future reuse.
- Final text, app UI, logos, CTA buttons, and proof labels must be rendered
  deterministically at export size, not left as AI-generated text.
- Generated photographic plates may be upscaled for testing, but reusable hero
  ad concepts should eventually regenerate source plates at 2x or higher when
  the concept graduates from test to production.

Cold-acquisition paid ads should default to three slides. A two-slide test is
allowed only when the hook and app proof are both instantly clear. Do not use
the six-slide organic carousel structure for paid acquisition.

Layout rules:

- Keep essential text, logos, and app UI inside the central safe area.
- The first slide must be readable at arm's length on a phone.
- Slide 2 must justify the swipe with a concrete evidence gap or receipt.
- Slide 3 must show the app, the check, or the app outcome.
- Do not hide the De-Influenced product until caption copy.
- Do not use tiny citation text as the main proof.

## Three-slide ad arc

| Slide | Role | Job | Typical visual | Copy shape |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Recognition | Stop the scroll by naming a real buyer pain or hype pattern. | Product/search/label/app-adjacent visual with strong editorial headline. | 4-10 words, specific and sharp. |
| 2 | Evidence gap | Show why the buyer needs a check. | Real label crop, claim crop, search wall, dose number, app result, or receipt-style card. | One gap, one number, one rule, or one receipt. |
| 3 | App resolution | Make De-Influenced the positive next step. | Real app screen, URL paste/search flow, verdict/results screen, or check summary. | App action + offer. |

Default spine:

1. Pain or hype pattern.
2. Evidence gap or receipt.
3. De-Influenced check and CTA.

The app must feel like relief from the decision problem, not an afterthought.

## Viewer personality routes

Choose one primary route before writing. A paid ad that tries to speak to every
consumer usually speaks to no one.

| Route | Buyer state | Emotional hook | Proof style | App resolution | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Logical Buyer | Wants a clear rule before trusting a claim. | "That claim needs a matching check." | Claim vs ingredient vs dose vs outcome. | De-Influenced checks product + dose + claim. | Vibes, broad outrage, wellness jokes. |
| Data Buyer | Wants numbers, receipts, and exactness. | "Hope is not a dose." | Dose visible, study fit, under-dosed/unknown result, testing evidence. | Show the app's check output or numbered result. | Saying "backed by science" without showing the check. |
| Hopeful Buyer | Has been pulled in by promises, rituals, influencers, or packaging. | "The marketing did its job." | The gap between how it feels and what is evidenced. | Check before the checkout. | Shaming the buyer or calling them gullible. |
| High-Level Buyer | Wants the quick verdict, not the full investigation. | "Before you buy, check one thing." | One rule, one app action, one plain-language takeaway. | Paste URL, product, or claim and get the check. | Dense tables, multi-step science lessons. |
| Overwhelmed Buyer | Has too many products, search results, reviews, and claims. | "Search gives options, not answers." | Search wall or product grid plus one evidence criterion. | De-Influenced turns the noise into a product check. | More options, more jargon, more open loops. |
| Premium Buyer | Is tempted by price, gifting, lifestyle cues, and expensive packaging. | "Premium is not proof." | Price, gold packaging, gifts, ritual, or luxury positioning vs dose/evidence. | Check whether the expensive product earns the claim. | Anti-luxury sneering or vague "price placebo" lines. |
| Safety-Worried Buyer | Wants reassurance but knows badges can be incomplete. | "A badge is not the whole check." | What was tested, when, by whom, and whether public proof exists. | De-Influenced checks the evidence without giving medical advice. | "Safe" or "unsafe" claims without receipts. |

## Route examples

### Logical Buyer

1. `Clinically studied is not product proof.`
2. `The ingredient, dose, audience, and outcome still have to match.`
3. `De-Influenced checks the claim before you buy. First check free.`

### Data Buyer

1. `Hope is not a dose.`
2. `If the amount is hidden, the claim is harder to verify.`
3. `Run the product through De-Influenced. First check free.`

### Hopeful Buyer

1. `The packaging did its job.`
2. `The gift, ritual, and glow do not prove the formula.`
3. `Check the evidence before checkout. First check free.`

### High-Level Buyer

1. `Before you buy, check one thing.`
2. `Does the product match the dose behind the claim?`
3. `Paste it into De-Influenced. First check free.`

### Overwhelmed Buyer

1. `Search gives options. Not answers.`
2. `Every product says it helps. That is not a check.`
3. `De-Influenced checks product + dose + claim. First check free.`

### Premium Buyer

1. `Premium is not proof.`
2. `A higher price does not create a better dose.`
3. `Check what is actually evidenced. First check free.`

### Safety-Worried Buyer

1. `Third-party tested sounds reassuring.`
2. `Ask what was tested, when, and whether the result is public.`
3. `De-Influenced checks the evidence trail. First check free.`

## Copy standard

Good paid-ad copy sounds like a useful interruption at the moment before a
purchase. It should be sharp, buyer-sided, and specific to the supplement
decision.

Passes:

- Names a concrete market pattern: hidden dose, unmatched claim, search wall,
  testing badge, gift, premium packaging, influencer proof, PubMed wallpaper.
- Gives the buyer a practical way to think.
- Makes the app the positive next step.
- Sounds like De-Influenced, not a generic wellness brand.

Fails:

- `Did you know gummies are not great?`
- `Supplements can be confusing.`
- `The wellness industry is full of lies.`
- `Don't get scammed.`
- `Science-backed supplements are everywhere.`

Sharpness test:

If the hook could be used unchanged by any generic supplement app, rewrite it.
If the hook names a specific buying mistake and the deck resolves it with the
De-Influenced check, it can move forward.

## Offer and CTA

Primary paid offer line:

> First check free.

Preferred CTA lines:

- `Run your first supplement check.`
- `Paste the product before you buy.`
- `Check the claim before checkout.`
- `Check it before you buy it.`

Avoid for cold acquisition:

- `Check for free.`
- `Free checks.`
- `Included in your plan.`
- Any wording that makes the offer sound aimed at already-registered users.

Conditional support lines:

- `No card needed.`
- `No ads or affiliate links.`
- `Plain-language evidence checks.`

Only use conditional support lines after verifying they match the current app,
signup flow, and landing page.

## Visual standard

The visual world is De-Influenced editorial investigation, not soft wellness and
not generic SaaS.

Use:

- Real app screens wherever the deck claims the app does something.
- Real product pages, labels, search walls, reports, or screenshots when rights
  and source context are clear.
- Dark editorial product/evidence scenes, newsroom desk textures, phone screens,
  label crops, receipt crops, and restrained parchment layouts.
- Navy, parchment, black, near-white, and Atomic Tangerine `#F76928`.
- One orange payload word or phrase when it earns attention.
- Deterministic text rendering for all final ad copy.

Avoid:

- Pastel wellness aesthetics.
- Decorative gradient blobs or abstract tech backgrounds.
- Fake ingredient tables, fake study titles, fake reviews, fake COAs, fake app
  screens, fake labels, or invented third-party logos.
- AI-generated text inside final ad imagery.
- Product screenshots used as evidence without source notes.
- Tiny unreadable receipt walls.

AI-generated images are allowed only as non-evidence background plates. They
must be text-free or contain only blurred/unreadable prop text. The real proof,
app UI, claims, numbers, labels, and CTA must be rendered deterministically from
approved copy and source assets.

## Reddit and buyer-language rule

Reddit and forums are signal, not publishable proof by default.

Use Reddit/VOC to find:

- The exact buyer state.
- The phrases shoppers use when they feel confused, hopeful, ripped off, or
  overwhelmed.
- The moment before the app would help.

Do not publish usernames, screenshots, or direct forum quotes in paid ads unless
usage rights, privacy, and platform risk have been reviewed. Transform forum
language into buyer-sided ad copy instead of making Reddit the subject.

## Claim and compliance rules

Meta ads must attack the claim environment, evidence gap, or checkout decision,
not the viewer's body or identity.

Safer:

- `Menopause supplement marketing can make products hard to compare.`
- `The claim still needs to match the product, dose, and evidence.`
- `A proprietary blend can hide the dose, which makes the claim harder to
  verify.`
- `Premium packaging is not evidence.`

Riskier or blocked unless reviewed:

- `Your symptoms are being exploited.`
- `This product does not work.`
- `This brand is scamming you.`
- `This supplement is unsafe.`
- `You need this if you are deficient/anxious/hormonal/sick.`

Specific rules:

- Do not diagnose the viewer.
- Do not imply the viewer has a medical condition or deficiency.
- Do not promise health outcomes from using De-Influenced.
- Do not call a brand, creator, or product fraudulent without sourced legal
  review.
- Do not say proprietary blends are always ineffective. Say the dose is hidden
  or unverifiable from the label when that is true.
- Do not say premium products are worthless. Say premium cues are not evidence.
- Named brands require a sourced claim and a risk note.
- No medical advice in either direction.

## App proof hierarchy

Best proof for slide 3:

1. Real app result screen or report verdict.
2. Real URL paste/search flow.
3. Real product + dose + claim check.
4. Real alternatives or comparison screen when the ad is about wasted money or
   product choice.
5. Simple product-method card only when the actual app screen is unavailable.

If the ad says De-Influenced checks something, the viewer should see the app
checking it or see the result of the check.

## Production contract

Production may happen in `de-influenced-studio`, Canva, HyperFrames, or the
Newsroom renderer, but the standard lives here.

Required output package:

- Three final PNG slides at `1080x1350`.
- Contact sheet.
- Phone-sized preview.
- Source/asset manifest.
- Caption and primary text draft.
- Landing URL.
- Risk notes.
- Test hypothesis.

Text rendering must be deterministic. Generated background plates are optional;
generated final text is not.

## Approval gates

Gate 1: concept approval

- Persona route.
- Hook.
- Offer line.
- Landing destination.
- Assets required.
- Risk notes.

Gate 2: phone preview approval

- Three slides rendered.
- Contact sheet reviewed.
- First slide readable at arm's length.
- Slide 2 earns the swipe.
- Slide 3 clearly shows the app/check.
- Offer matches the landing page.

Gate 3: upload package approval

- Final PNGs.
- Primary text.
- Headline.
- CTA.
- Destination URL.
- Naming convention.
- Final risk note.

No paid ad auto-publishes. No batch render of ten ads should happen before Liz
has signed off this template standard and the first representative deck.

## Acceptance checklist

A paid Meta carousel is ready for Liz review only when all of these are true:

- It is 3 slides or fewer.
- It is `1080x1350`, `4:5`.
- It names one primary viewer route.
- Slide 1 names a specific buyer pain or hype pattern.
- Slide 2 shows a concrete evidence gap, receipt, rule, or number.
- Slide 3 makes De-Influenced the practical resolution.
- The CTA uses `First check free.` unless Liz approves a different offer.
- The deck does not shame the buyer.
- The deck does not diagnose the viewer.
- The deck does not invent evidence, app UI, labels, reviews, studies, or COAs.
- Named claims have source notes.
- The landing page matches the offer and app promise.
- The phone preview is readable without zooming.
- The final output includes PNGs, contact sheet, manifest, caption, and risk
  notes.

If any item fails, the deck is not gold-standard yet.
