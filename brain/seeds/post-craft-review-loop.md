# Post craft review loop

Save into the brain as slug `post-craft-review-loop`, kind `formats`.

This doc is the reusable quality loop for De-Influenced posts. A post is not
ready because it has a script, a caption, or a render. It is ready when the
hook, message, visual craft, asset truth, platform fit, and app resolver all
survive the loop below.

Use this for every post that moves beyond a throwaway fast check, and always
for app-demo, HyperFrames, carousel, satirical receipt, and hero edits.

## The rule

Review the post as a viewer, not as the person who made it.

The viewer should understand three things without effort:

1. what pain point is being named
2. what evidence check resolves it
3. why De-Influenced is the natural next action

If any one of those is unclear, the post goes back through the loop.

## Loop states

Score every gate as:

- `green`: publishable
- `amber`: usable only if the listed fix is made
- `red`: not publishable; rewrite or rebuild this part

Every amber or red must produce a concrete revision instruction. Do not save
generic notes like "needs work". Name the exact fix: missing asset, scene
range, hook rewrite, app-demo replacement, proof gap, or pixel issue to
rerender. After the revision, rerun only the gates affected by the change, then
rerun the final whole-post pass.

Every non-ready pass must create a next action. If the decision is `pending`,
`revise`, or `blocked`, name either the concrete revision to make or the single
asset needed next. Do not save a status-only review log.

If multiple gates are amber or red, each gate needs its own fix. Do not hide an
app-resolver problem, a hook problem, and a pixel problem inside one combined
note.

Keep the pass history visible for the selected route. Each pass should show the
decision, blockers, required revision, next asset, and artifact or stills
evidence so the loop proves improvement over time.

Every review pass belongs to the selected route. Do not save an unscoped review
when routes exist, and do not let a pass from another route unlock Gate 2 for
the active route.

## Gate 2 enforcement

For Tier 2, Tier 3, and Tier 4 routes, Gate 2 approval is blocked until the
selected route has a review pass marked `ready`, every gate is `green`, and the
review includes the rendered artifact path, contact sheet or reviewed stills,
plus a proof pack for the hook, message, app resolver, CTA, pixel pass, and
final viewer action.

Every named gate must be present and green. A partial legacy gate object is not
ready, and a review with any remaining `Next asset needed` is not ready.

Gate 2 is also blocked while required selected-route or story-level assets are
still `needed` or `generating`. Supply, select, or waive them before final
approval. Story-level required assets still block legacy Gate 2 stories without
a selected route.

A ready review pass cannot be saved while non-review required assets are still
open. Review-generated `Review asset:` requests may be waived by a later ready
pass when they are no longer the blocker, including open `needed` or
`generating` legacy story-level review requests.

Tier 1 fast-lane checks may still use the loop, but the app should not block
their Gate 2 approval unless the route has been intentionally escalated.

## Gate 1: Format lock

The format must be decided before hook polishing.

Check:

- Is this TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, or a native split?
- Is the production tier explicit?
- Does the route fit the job: visibility, trust, or conversion?
- Is the lightest valid format being used, unless a heavier one is justified?
- Are Liz-owned assets explicit?
- Are agent-created assets allowed, or would they weaken credibility?

Pass condition:

> The post has one platform-native shape and one production job. It is not
> trying to be a TikTok, carousel, product audit, and app ad at the same time.

## Gate 2: First-frame hook

The hook must work before the viewer has decided to stay.

For video/Reels/TikTok:

- The visual hook must be readable with sound off in the first 1.5 seconds.
- On-screen hook text should usually be 3-8 words.
- The spoken hook can be longer than the on-screen hook.
- No preamble, greeting, logo-first open, or abstract setup.
- The first image must carry the problem, not decorate it.

For carousels:

- Slide 1 must be 10 words or fewer.
- Slide 2 must create the reason to swipe.

Pass condition:

> A stranger can tell what problem is being named before they scroll away.

## Gate 3: Message spine

The whole post must reduce to one sentence:

> This helps the viewer with [pain] by showing them [check].

Check:

- Is there one pain point, not a bundle of related complaints?
- Is there one main check, not four lessons crammed into one post?
- Does each beat move pain -> proof -> product -> promise?
- Does the payoff close the hook promise?

Pass condition:

> If one sentence cannot describe the post, the post is overstuffed.

## Gate 4: App as resolver

For conversion posts, the app is not a logo at the end. It is the practical
resolution to the viewer's pain.

Check:

- Does De-Influenced enter before the final CTA, ideally around the midpoint?
- Is the app shown doing a job, not just being named?
- Is the app job concrete: paste product, paste claim, check dose, check claim,
  compare evidence, or save money?
- Does the CTA name the benefit before the action?
- Is there only one CTA?

Preferred CTA shapes:

- `Paste the product or claim into De-Influenced before you buy.`
- `Check the claim before you buy. First analysis free.`
- `Evidence, not influence. Check yours on the site.`

Pass condition:

> The viewer should think, "that would reduce the work I have to do," not
> "this brand has an app."

## Gate 5: Asset truth

Use real receipts when the credibility depends on reality.

Check:

- Screenshots, product pages, labels, PubMed pages, price crops, and app results
  must be real if they are being used as evidence.
- Generated images may clarify a concept but must never pretend to be proof.
- Do not create fake app results, fake PubMed screens, fake product labels, or
  generic supplement bottles for receipt-led posts.
- If a screenshot is used as environmental proof, say that clearly. Do not use
  it as product-efficacy proof.

Pass condition:

> Every visual tells the truth about what it is: receipt, demo, concept, or
> brand card.

## Gate 6: Voice and compliance

The voice is buyer-sided: mate who did the homework.

Check:

- The joke, if any, sits on a receipt.
- The buyer is never mocked.
- The claim or marketing move is the target.
- The line sounds natural spoken aloud.
- No broad medical advice, cure/prevent/treat language, or unsupported efficacy
  claims.
- No invented intent: avoid "they knew", "they hid", "they tricked you" unless
  directly evidenced.
- The fair position is stated where the topic is sensitive.

Pass condition:

> The viewer feels helped and respected, while the marketing claim is held to a
> clearer standard.

## Gate 7: Pixel and motion pass

For HyperFrames and video, check the frame like a finished post.

Check:

- 9:16 output is 1080x1920 unless a platform requires otherwise.
- Top-third hook is readable and not hidden by app controls.
- Bottom-third copy has a legibility gradient or clean background.
- Screenshot crops are large enough to understand on a phone.
- No more than one dominant text idea per frame.
- No text overlaps busy product grids without darkening, blurring, or a reading
  zone.
- Captions fit inside the safe area and do not cover the key receipt.
- Brand palette is consistent: navy, parchment, orange, green only unless the
  route justifies a new colour.
- Motion supports comprehension: slow pan, clean highlight, app demo, CTA. No
  frantic zooms to manufacture energy.

Pass condition:

> The post is readable on a phone at arm's length with the sound off.

## Gate 8: Platform-native pass

TikTok:

- Keep the current lane stable for two weeks.
- One pain point, one check, recognisable structure.
- Hook in the first 1.5 seconds.
- Long SEO caption can carry extra context.
- Do not judge the post on day-one performance.

Instagram:

- More saveable, polished, and keyword-led.
- Carousel or Reel must still sound like Liz, not a corporate explainer.
- Caption should answer the search query clearly.
- Hashtags are secondary to keywords.

Pass condition:

> The same insight may become multiple posts, but each platform gets its own
> native build.

## Final whole-post question

Before Gate 2 approval, answer this in one line:

> What exactly does the viewer know or do differently after this post?

If the answer does not point back to a useful buyer behaviour or the app's
claim-checking job, revise before publishing.

Do not ask Liz for "more assets" generally. Ask for the single asset that moves
the next gate from amber to green.

When `Next asset needed` is filled on a review pass, the app should create a
single Liz-owned `Needs me` asset request for the selected route. When a later
pass is marked `ready`, any open review-generated asset request for that route
should be waived so stale blockers do not remain in the inbox.
